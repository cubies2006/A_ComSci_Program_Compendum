XXVII. Movies of Great Influence To Me From Which I Can Relate
  1. BRAVEHEART

         This movie is very historical and has a very beautiful scenery. Because the movie deals with how William Wallis, an ordinary village man, defend Scotland from the British invaders. Even though he was executed in the end, but the national conciousness of his countrymen had finally awaken and defend their country against their invaders. The moral of the film is that if you took advantage of the rights of other people and abused it, one day they'll ignite a revolution.

  2. THE COUNT OF MONTE CRISTO

         This movie is all about love, betrayal, and revenge. This movie deals with the betrayal of Mondego to Edmund Dantes by accusing the later as Napoleon's agent. He was been brought to Chateau D'If, where he had suffered many hardships for many years. But he is very greatful to meet Abbe Faria, who taught him all the disciplines and swordsmanship turning him into a civilized man. When Faria died, Edmund uses this opportunity to escape and venture to a place where he had acquired the treasure of Monte Cristo. He returned as the Count of Monte Cristo to seek revenge for those who did him wrong. Later, he found out that his false best-friend had married Mercedes, who is Edmund's fiance. And they had a son who Edmund taught that his father was Mondego, but actually his own son. He successfully avenged all his enemies. In the end, he, Mercedes, and their son got united. So therefore, the moral lesson of this film is that there are no permanent friends nor permanent enemies.

  3. TITANIC

         This story is about love and tragedy that man can't escape with. In each level of the ship represents the social classes during 1900's. In the lower part, the poor class. While in the middle part, the middle class. And in the upper part, the rich or the first class. In the film, it features how the ship Titanic got sink. The story added some flavor with the story of Jack and Rose. Rose, who is engaged to Cal, get to meet Jack when she attempted to suicide on the ship because she's not happy with her luxurious lifestyle. With Jack, she became very happy and very fell in love with him. But the tragedy made them separate forever. The moral lesson of this film is that man can't escape tragedy when his or her time comes.
