Origin: Namor is a mutant hybrid of Homo Sapiens and the off-shoot human race called Homo Mermanus.

Prince Namor is actually the very first character introduced into the Marvel Universe way back in 1939. He carried a successful title for 42 issues in the Golden Age of comics as well as appearing in other titles. Just a couple of issues into the Silver Age, Namor was brought back in the pages of Fantastic Four #4. Sometimes a villain, many more times a hero, the Submariner has always been a very important character in the Marvel Universe and eventually appeared in Tales To Astonish for over 30 issues before receiving his own title for over 70 and again for over 50. While the hot-head Namor is not exactly a great hero to combine forces with, he has teamed up with just about every character in the Marvel Universe including Spider-Man.

 

 

 

The Submariner and Spider-Man has met up only a few relative times over their careers. One early memorable one was in Daredevil #77 in a story that was concluded in Submariner #40. Namor made his Marvel Team-Up debut in issue #14 and had a major battle issue with Spidey in Submariner #69. They met again in the pages of Amazing Spider-Man #211, 214 & 215 where they battled then joined forces against the Frightful Four. Namor has been a founding member of the Defenders and can be seen alongside Spider-Man as well as his Defender teammates in issues #52 & 107-109. The Submariner has been a prominent character for over 60 years and continues to be a part of the action in the Marvel Universe.
