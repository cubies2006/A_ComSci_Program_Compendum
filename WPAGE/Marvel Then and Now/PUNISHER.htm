News and Rumors:

June 16, 2004: The Punisher DVD will be coming out September 7, 2004. The disc will feature a 2.35 anamorphic transfer, along with DD 5.1 and DTS Surround tracks. Extras on tap include the "War Journal: On the Set of The Punisher" making-of featurette, "Army of One" character history featurette (with an interview with comic writer Garth Ennis), "Drawing Blood" artwork featurette (interview with artist Tim Bradstreet), "Keepin' It Real" stunt featurette and a selection of theatrical trailers.

February 21, 2004: Yahoo! Movies has posted the new trailer for The Punisher in the QuickTime, Windows Media and Real formats.

January 28, 2004: The official Punisher site has been updated. "The site has included Volume III: Saints & Sinners, which gives a detailed background on the type of person Mr. Howard Saint is in the public eye. It also includes a cool delta training first person shooter game found on the laptop computer in the far corner, and a detailed link to the FBI website, with information on the case to bring down Howard Saint himself." Thanks to a Superherohype.com scooper.

December 28, 2003: Rebbeca Romijn-Stamos sat down with Wizard Magazine and talked a little bit about her role in April's Punisher: "Joan's this down and out recovering drug addict just trying to get her life back together. She lives in this shabby tenement building that Frank Castle [portrayed by Thomas Jane] moves into as he is just getting over the death of his family and turning into the Punisher. He's this mysterious guy doing all this stuff in his apartment and none of us who live in the building can figure out who he is or what it is that he's doing. And I think my character related to him. I identify with him as somebody who just doesn't care about life anymore because he's lost his family and he's drinking heavily." Thanks to Wizard Magazine and Superherohype.com.

December 4, 2003: A site as been created for the Punisher movie soundtrack!

The featured track is a song by the band Edgewater, an outstanding band from Dallas that was recently signed by Wind-up records. You may recall Wind-up produced the soundtrack for last year's Daredevil, which featured the Evanescence song that shot them into fame. You can expect the same from Edgewater. The soundtrack will be in stores on March 23. Thanks to Superherohype for the heads up!

November 4, 2003: Apple has posted the new trailer for 'The Punisher' in quicktime! The trailer shows off Thomas Jane in full-on crazy murder mode as The Punisher, as well as providing a first look at John Travolta as Howard Saint and Kevin Nash as The Russian! Click here for the punishment!

November 3, 2003: New trailer on Access Hollywood tonight! Check your local listings. I'm sure a online version will be available shortly after.

October 23, 2003: According to a report on Aint It Cool News from the recent Winter NATO report, we may be able to expect the next trailer for 'The Punisher' by mid-November. Thanks to Countingdown for the heads up!

October 22, 2003: From WWE.com: "The last time WWE fans saw [Kevin] Nash was at SummerSlam. Since then, he's completed work on a feature film, "The Punisher," starring Thomas Jane, John Travolta and Rebecca Romijn-Stamos. Nash plays a Russian assassin trying to kill the Punisher. The movie is due to hit theaters on April 16. "Anybody who reads the comic book will know the Russian assassin," Nash said. Nash said he wasn't in any scenes with Travolta, but "working with Thomas was fun. He's a really good actor.""

October 17, 2003: Artisan Entertainment has sent us the new, kick ass, poster for The Punisher (see above)! The poster comes one week early before it hits theater walls. In other news, The Punisher has officially wrapped up production of the film.

October 14, 2003: Expect to see a new Punisher poster some time next week! I can't wait!

October 1, 2003: DarkHorizons scooper sends in a report about the Punisher script: "I got a look at the script, and I'll tell you right now, Garth Ennis fans are in for a big treat. There are big portions of the first story arc, which I think is "welcome home frank" in this script, and it's cool. I enjoyed the script very much - the russian, bumpo, joan, all there. Jonathan Hensleigh also brings in his own voice, and they are trying to make Tampa WAY more dangerous than it actually is. I don't want to give anything away, but I will say that Saint is written as a very flawed character, very human. He's not pure evil, but he does do evil things. Castle is not the comic book character we all know, but he is not all that different either. He's not a one man wrecking crew so much as, like Saint, a flawed person trying to make sense of the loss of his family, and his way to do this is to kill a lot of people. BTW, His father (Roy Scheider) wasn't in this version of the script, only mentioned when he picks up his father's 45's, and parker shotguns. The big family death scene I saw is not in this script, and I'm afraid they'll cut him out of the film, which would be a shame. They are nearly done with shooting. Only a few more weeks. I believe [John] Travolta wraps in a week, and [Thomas] Jane may already be done, actually. Not sure about that one." Thanks to DarkHorizons.

September 24, 2003: Just a small note: The official Punisher website has been updated with new stuff. Click the link above.

September 18, 2003: According to The Hollywood Reporter, Marcus Johns has now joined Artisan Entertainment's 'The Punisher', where he will play Will Castle, Frank Castle's son.

August 28, 2003: DarkHorizons has post what they claim may be the opening scene in The Punisher:

"I have seen what I believe is going to be the opening. If not, an important flashback. An "undercover" gay eurotrash Frank Castle ordering some punks around with a lisp and a fat cigarette, then gets shot by his partner. Based on what I saw, I don't know if it's supposed to be an accident, or if his partner is "dirty". Then a bunch of FBI guys bust in and do a lot of shouting and gun pointing. Castle shows up at an outdoor press conference in downtown Tampa to confront his partner, they talk about him on the news too, he's wearing the skull shirt, but he has a shirt buttoned over it. Kinda stupid. There is also a scene of him talking to his son, and get this, his son says "I liked you better with blonde hair" He replies " I'm a Castle, we've always had black hair." He then continues to talk about the Castle family, how they have been moving from place to place for years, and how this move for the family will be the last. I suppose that's supposed to be cryptic, since they all get gunned down in short order.

August 21, 2003: Former WWE icon, Hulk Hogan called in to the Bubba the Love Sponge radio show recently. He noted Kevin Nash's haircut and the fact that it was for a part he has in the coming "Punisher" movie. He said that Nash will play the part of a wrestler in the movie, and that he himself will have cameo appearance in the movie as well.

Ausust 19, 2003: According to RajaWWF, The movie "The Punisher" is in filming as we speak, as we reported earlier. Kevin Nash will be featured in the movie, possibly as a villain. As to exactly what Nash's role will be we do not know, but we do know that producers wanted him to come in with his hair cut shorter. We will be sure to keep you posted. Editor's Note: Nash's role is suggested to be Tombstone, we'll keep you posted.

August 18, 2003: From IGN FilmForce:

According to IGN FilmForce's longtime reliable Punisher source, the [Tom] Nowicki rumor isn't true. Tombstone WILL be in The Punisher - referred to only as Lincoln, mind you - but, as of presstime, our source hadn't gotten back to us about who has been cast in the role. Earlier bogus rumors had mentioned Kurt Russell for the part.

August 14, 2003: Mexican Soap Star Eduardo Yanez has joined the cast as a Cuban gangster according to The Hollywood Reporter.

August 12, 2003: Roy Scheider has joined the cast. Comics2Film/CBR News has learned that Scheider will play Frank Castle's father in the movie. Our source was unsure if the lead character would interact with his father in present day or in flashback.

August 11, 2003: Tom Nowicki is rumoured to have been cast as Lonnie Lincoln (aka Tombstone).

August 3, 2003: Superherohype.com reports on filming down in Tampa, some spoilers ahead: "I got some Punisher news from the St. Pete Times. The movie started shooting in Tampa Sat. It was filmed at the AmSouth building in downtown Tampa. The story says that The Punisher kills two of Saint's henchmen named Dante & Spoon. And they filmed a scene where they threw fake money from the AmSouth building and had people try the castch it. It rainned here and delayed some of the shooting. It also says that [John] Travolta won't be shooting anything for several weeks. The only moive star seen was Jane. But they said That Jimmy Smits was seen around town. He is staying at the nearby Hyatt. Why he was here is a mystery to everyone."
