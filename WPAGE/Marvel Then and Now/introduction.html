X-2: X-Men United

The summer kicks off with the sequel of one of the long-awaited comic book adaptations in recent history. While the first X-Men managed to amaze avid fans and average moviegoers alike, it didn't quite meet my expectations. However, "X-Men United" definitely delivers, packing double the action and double the special effects. Being the geek that I am, they might as well have titled it 2X.

I don't really trust trailers that much; they're created to manipulate watchers for pure marketing goals. From the first impression of the X-2 trailer, it seemed like Hollywood traded the plot for meaningless fight scenes and hot girls in uniform. However, after seeing the movie, it became all clear; the plot is still there and the fight scenes are meaningful. Oh yeah, and the hot girls in uniform are there, too. *grin*

Yeah… we have more cool characters, awesome action sequences, dazzling special effects (although a bit too shiny at times) and, finally, a pretty good storyline. The first "X-Men," building the foundation for the series, was sort of bland. This one was definitely more exciting, especially with the thought of X-Men uniting to clean up some fucked up human filth! Oops… However, I was disappointed with Mystique. No, she does have some good moves, but her character just isn't as dark as before. She's no longer… a mystique.

The story goes like this: A military scientist seeking vengeance for his son's death devises ways to control the mutant's minds, to sway them for his own evil purposes; mainly, to rid the world of the mutant presence. The mutants, good and bad, unite to face this mortal threat. The story builds up to the very sad but hopeful ending.

Yes, there is a hint somewhere close to the end. It seems we're looking at yet another X-Men movie. With many comic-based movies out there, from Spider-man to The Hulk, it looks like the real war is about to take place… on-screen and off it. Anyway, if the next X-Men installment is, at the least, as good as this one, I'm there.
