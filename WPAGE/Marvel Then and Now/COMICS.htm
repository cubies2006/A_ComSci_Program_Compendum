BIOGRAPHY - Page 1
Being the daughter of Chinese immigrants, she was named Jubilation for the joy her people had felt upon coming to America. Due to the father’s job as a bank director, the family had a large income and they ended up living in Beverly Hills, with young Jubilation entering prep school, where her talent for gymnastics was discovered. However, Jubilation used her agility for not so noble things, with her and her best friend spending most of their time at the mall de jour, making petty robberies, making their escape on their rollerblades. It wasn’t for the money, just for the sake of doing it, or, as Jubilee put it, she wasn’t proud of doing it, just of how they did it. One day, though, Jubilee was trapped in a dead-end alley by a pair of rent-a-cops. Fearing juvenile detention, not to mention her parents‘ reaction, she panicked and her pyrotechnic powers manifested, hitting full blast to destroy the entire alley. Her first instinct was to run to the beach, where she tried to keep her hands wet so that it would never happen again. [Generation X #24]


Jubilee told her parents about her strange abilities, but it didn’t seem to be a problem, as she soon learned how to control it to a limited degree, keeping her powers from activating without her realizing it. Not much later, though, the Lees were killed in what seemed to be a car accident on Mulholland Drive. At the time, Jubilee had been once more at the shopping mall with her friends. Arriving back home, Jubilation found the police waiting for her to inform her of the accident and she was handed over to another couple named Lee from the neighborhood. Jubilee didn’t like it there and ran away as soon as possible, returning to the mall, which now seemed to be her only home. [Wolverine (2nd series) #72]


Jubilee stayed there for weeks, either living from stealing or earning some money by performing with her fireworks. The rent-a-cops, though, were annoyed by her constantly outrunning them and eventually called in some mutant hunters, the M-Squad. The same day the mall was visited by the female contingent of the Australian X-Men group, Storm, Rogue, Dazzler and Psylocke. Jubilee was the sole witness to their unique way of entrance, through one of Gateway’s teleportation portals, and, admiring the beautiful

four ladies, she secretly followed them through the mall. When the M-Squad attacked the young girl, they were easily defeated by the X-Men, who then left the scene exactly the same way they had arrived. Jubilee was intrigued by there being other people with super-powers and, since Gateway deliberately left the portal open long enough, for reasons known only to him, Jubilee jumped through after them. [Uncanny X-Men #244]


Finding herself in the middle of the Australian desert, Jubilee became somewhat scared and wondered what the X-Men might do to her. Therefore, she took up secretly living in the X-Men's base, an abandoned town in the Australian Outback that had formerly been used by the Reavers, scrounging food from the pantries and making a small hidey-hole for herself to live in. Gateway was the only one to know of her, but didn‘t alert the team to her presence. She was almost exposed, when Nanny and the Orphan Maker attacked the base and saw Jubilee, though in the confusion of the battle she could slip away before any of the X-Men became aware of her. [Uncanny X-Men Annual #15, Uncanny X-Men #247-248]


The X-Men might have found her soon enough but, almost immediately after Jubilee arrived, the team began to dissolve, some apparently dying and others leaving due to personal matters. When the Reavers returned to reclaim their base from the X-Men, the remaining members decided to use the Siege Perilous as an escape route. Psylocke, Havok, Dazzler and Colossus entered the magical doorway and were taken to new lives, unaware of the secret houseguest they left behind. When Wolverine returned from his brief leave of absence, he was taken by surprise and the Reavers placed him on a cross and "crucified" him in fire and blood. Jubilee was horrified by this scenario but also wanted to help the strange man, and at night she helped him to get free and took him to her hideout below the city.





There, Jubilee tended to Logan’s wounds and also stayed by his side when he became delusional. Critically wounded, Wolverine was dragged through the desert by Jubilee until they reached Sydney, where they then proceeded on a ship to the island of Madripoor. [Uncanny X-Men #251-253]


Jubilee became something of a kid sidekick to Logan, looking after him as his healing factor dealt with his wounds, and they formed a father-daughter bond. When they reached Madripoor, the partners met Lady Mandarin, who was actually Psylocke brain-washed and in a new, Asian body. Jubilee was captured by the Mandarin, Psylocke's controller, and dressed up like a China doll. In desperation, Jubilation unleashed an unprecedented amount of plasmoid power from her body, completely demolishing the house she was in at the time. This incident made Jubilee extremely wary in the future of ever pushing the limits of her powers, as she was afraid of hurting people again.


Psylocke was eventually freed from the Mandarin's control when she used her psychic knife on Wolverine, creating a psychic backlash, as in his memories she saw herself how she was supposed to be. Now a third member being added to their traveling party, Jubilation was not immediately trusting of Betsy and the two of them were heavily at odds for their first few months together. However, for the sake of Wolverine, they worked together and the trio shared several other adventures in Madripoor. [Uncanny X-Men #257-258]
